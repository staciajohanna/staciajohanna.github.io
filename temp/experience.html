18.065 Matrix Methods in Data Analysis, Signal Processing, and Machine Learning Final Group Project
Spring 2020

Comparing Regression Methods for Predicting COVID-19 Response

Topic : Machine learning
Skills used : Python, Tensorflow, Keras
In 18.065, the students can do any project related to any of the class materials or other materials related to linear algebra or statistics. Since our group have many international experience, we are interested in how regression methods that we learned in class can be used to predict how certain factors in different countries may affect their death per capita. The regression methods we use for this project are linear regression, lasso regression, neural networks, and principal component analysis.
For this project, my main work focused on neural networks. The relevant parts on the paper are 2.4 Background, 4.3.4 Modeling, 5.3 Result and Analysis. I trained ~80 different neural network models to find the best result based on root mean squared error (RMSE) and tuned different components of the models, such as number of layers and nodes, optimizer, activation functions, etc.

Undergraduate Researcher/Game Developer - MIT Teaching Systems Lab
June 2018 - August 2018 || Cambridge, MA

Learning Science Playground

Topic : educational game, web development
Skills used : HTML, CSS, JavaScript, AirConsole
I joined Learning Science Playground project through MIT UROP (Undergraduate Research Opportunities Program). In the project, I collaborated with 7 members to create interactive tools for pre-service teacher education in Woodrow Wilson Academy. Each of us have our own individual projects, in which all of the project are based on the chapters in the book The ABCs of How We Learn by Daniel L. Schwartz, Jessica M. Tsang, and Kristen P. Blair. My project focused on chapter V, which is Visualization.
I built the game from the start, including initial potential user interview, design, development, and weekly testing. I devised and implemented the idea and mechanics of the game, and revised it based on the feedback from the play tester.
The game is inspired from Jackbox games, in which multiple people can use their personal controllers/smartphones as the controller, and have television as the central screen. As seen from the gif, in my game, the central screen is the laptop, while the phones are the controllers. Since I used web platform for my game, any phone that can access the internet can be used, so it is accessible for most people.
